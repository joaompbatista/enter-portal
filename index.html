📋 Project Context

Enter is a Berlin-based climate-tech company with €35 million in funding, working to make energy-neutral buildings a reality for homeowners across Germany. The company aggregates and analyses building data to help homeowners reduce energy costs and increase property value through targeted retrofitting measures such as heat pumps, insulation, solar PV, and window replacement.

This portal demonstrates a data analytics layer built around Enter's operational model. It covers the full lifecycle of a retrofitting project, from contract signature through execution to homeowner satisfaction, with a particular focus on forecast accuracy and CSAT scores, which are central KPIs for the Senior Data Analyst role.

🗃️ Data Model Overview

The database follows a star schema with 4 dimension tables, 1 calendar table, and 4 fact tables. Dimension tables describe entities (crews, homeowners, properties, contracts). Fact tables capture measurable events (projects, energy readings, forecasts, CSAT surveys).

🗄️ Tables

All 9 tables in the Enter Portal data model.

TableTypeColumnsDescription
dim_date Dimension 8 Calendar table covering 2023–2025. Used to support time-based filtering across all fact tables.
dim_crews Dimension 5 Internal retrofitting crews, each led by a named crew lead and specialised in a specific intervention type (e.g. Heat Pumps, Solar PV).
dim_homeowners Dimension 7 Homeowners who have engaged with Enter. Includes contact details, city, and acquisition channel.
dim_properties Dimension 9 Physical properties registered for retrofitting. Records property type, construction year, floor area, and energy class before and after intervention.
dim_contracts Dimension 7 Contracts signed between Enter and homeowners. Links a property and a crew, and defines contract type, value, and status.
fact_projects Fact 13 Core fact table tracking individual retrofitting projects. Captures start and end dates, actual vs budgeted cost, CO2 savings, energy savings percentage, and project status.
fact_energy_readings Fact 7 Quarterly energy consumption readings per property. Breaks down total consumption into heating and electricity, with associated cost in euros.
fact_forecasts Fact 9 Energy consumption and savings forecasts produced per property and crew. Compares forecasted values against actuals to calculate forecast accuracy percentage, a key KPI for crew leads.
fact_csat_surveys Fact 12 Homeowner satisfaction surveys collected after deep dive sessions, forecast reviews, and progress updates with crew leads. Captures scores for data insights quality, forecast accuracy, communication, and overall satisfaction. Includes a flag indicating whether the crew lead used the forecasting product as the primary source of truth.
